Blog #1: Let there be light

The first day - light was created.

Hello, world!

Brief background: I am a mid-level civil engineer who wants to shift in tech since this is my childhood dream and have been studying the concepts of programming and software development since the start of year 2022, but it seems like I still do not know SHIT. Excuse the language, but I just want to express how I really feel. It feels like I wasted precious amount of time chasing through tails and achieving nothing in value.

I scrambled through different free online resources to learn as much as I can but it seems that this is a bad way of learning and I'm stuck in what people call a "tutorial hell".

To slowly kick myself out of this endless loop, I decided to finally make my own blog-slash-portfolio website. Basically a place online wherein I'll dump and put anything tech-related that I create.

I'm naming this project "Project Genesis" which is inspired from the name of the first book of the Hebrew Bible and the Christian Old Testament. It is an account of the creation of the world -- and literally my very first personal and "real world" project coming to tech. I somewhat view this project as creating my own world, hence the name. This website will be initially made with plain HTML only, no CSS, no whatever. I will improve this site from time to time as I review and solidify my knowledge in web development fundamentals. Hopefully, I can add features that will make my website stand out among the crowd.

My main goal is to be a very competent software engineer but I face a big challenge: I do not have any formal education related to computer science, computer engineering, IT, whatever. Nevertheless, I have heard stories of people who made it without a degree and dedicated myself to follow their steps. That does not mean that I am not aware of the people on the other side of the coin who failed and did not make it, which at the same time, serve my inspiration and my day-to-day reality check to remind me to take this very seriously because the stakes are too damn high and I cannot afford more failures (I consider myself as one, as of the moment. Hope the feeling will pass).

Currently, these are the topics that I am studying:

  • Google Data Analytics*
  • Google IT Support*
  • App Academy Open - Web Development
  • Core Java

Because of the uncertainty brought by having no computer degree/background and job competition, I am just grabbing and learning whatever I can. More entries means more chances of winning, right? Sounds crazy, I know. But sometimes we need a little bit of crazy in life.

* - free Coursera courses sponsored by Globe Telecom. Thank you guys!

I am currently unemplyed as of typing. Last night, I cannot sleep simply just because of the pressure and the noise behind my head that kept telling "what if I can't make it?". Later I realized that I learn best by doing and not by mindlessly following an endless loop of tutorials and solving Leetcode questions, hence the conception of this website. So, here we go. Day one of Project Genesis. Let there be light. Now I'm enlightened on what I really want to do and what I really want to be in life...

I want to change the world for the better, even by a very little drop, through software.

And yup I won't lie, the economy here in the Philippines is slowly failing and I need to upskill and be prepared for whatever challenge may come in the future. After all, the needs of my family comes first.

As breadwinners, we are expected to win. So that's what I'll do.

I will make it happen.
